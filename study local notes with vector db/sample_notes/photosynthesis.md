# Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light
energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. It occurs in two stages: the
light-dependent reactions and the light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle).

## Light-dependent reactions

These take place in the thylakoid membranes of the chloroplast. Chlorophyll absorbs light,
exciting electrons that travel through the electron transport chain. Water is split
(photolysis), releasing oxygen as a by-product. The energy is captured as ATP (via
chemiosmosis / ATP synthase) and NADPH. Net inputs: light, water; net outputs: O2, ATP, NADPH.

## Light-independent reactions (Calvin cycle)

These occur in the stroma and do not directly require light. Using the ATP and NADPH from
the light-dependent reactions, CO2 is fixed by the enzyme RuBisCO and reduced through a
series of steps to form glucose (G3P). Net inputs: CO2, ATP, NADPH; net output: glucose.

## Overall equation

6 CO2 + 6 H2O + light energy → C6H12O6 + 6 O2
